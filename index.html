Flood Zone Segmentation Using U-Net and Blended Loss Functions

December 2025 ‚Ä¢ Co-researchers: Oleg Ponyatyshyn, Vaibhav Dixit ‚Ä¢ Advisor: Dr. John Davis

Read Paper

Developed a U-Net convolutional neural network to automatically map flooded areas from satellite imagery, addressing a critical need for rapid disaster assessment. The study evaluated how different combinations of Dice and Focal loss functions affect segmentation accuracy on imbalanced data.

Key Findings:

  • Optimal Configuration: 0.75 Dice / 0.25 Focal loss achieved the best balance, improving water IoU from 0.52 to 0.66 and cloud IoU from 0.68 to 0.75 compared to pure Dice loss
  • Practical Insight: Pure Dice loss achieved 91.5% water accuracy but only 0.52 IoU‚Äîrevealing that high accuracy doesn't guarantee reliable flood mapping due to systematic overestimation
  • Implementation Gap: Identified that 92% accurate models still face deployment challenges when emergency managers need near-perfect reliability for life-or-death decisions
  • Technical Achievement: Demonstrated that modest focal weighting (25%) reduces false positives without destabilizing segmentation, while aggressive weighting (50%) degrades performance
